Fracture

An information poster to visualize my rhythm game scores during quarantine.

A data visualization project to collect and visualize data from activities in our lives.

Fracture is a fascinating project about collecting data from our lives and visualizing them in poster form. As COVID swiped through the world, classes moved online, allowing me to return home to Taiwan. However, I was required to quarantine for 14 days. During those time, I collected all the scores from three different rhythm games I played, Cytus II, Arcaea, and Lanota. Then, I compiled the data and transformed them into a compelling narrative.

Timeline.

Spring 2020

Skill.

Data visualization, information design.

Software.

Illustrator, Google Sheets.

Contribution.

Individual project.

Create immersion by utilizing the entire poster margin.

I aimed to add immersion, so I full-bleed everything and used the entire poster as a scale. The different portions represent the time played for each game, and detailed information lies within each tile. In addition, I included a short description, key legend, and an image of the physical gameplay to provide context for the audience.
