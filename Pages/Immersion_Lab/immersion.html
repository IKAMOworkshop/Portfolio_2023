Immersion
Lab.

Exploring different emerging tech to expand the possibility of design process.

Exploring the addition of 3D to an immersive experience and building prototypes with new tools to test.

I took a series of courses in the ArtCenter’s Immersion Lab, which allowed us to thoroughly explore new technologies and software that can expand the traditional design process that we’re all familiar with. The final goal is not to create a marketable product but to quickly learn, experiment, and prototype.

We explored the potential of what 3D can add to an immersive experience with new tools that can speed up the process and play-tested the prototypes. We also discussed developing tech (AI images, computational design, etc.) and their impact on design and the world.

Read about the Immersion Lab.

Testing with new software to break the boundary of brainstorming and iterating.

Pairing two unorthodox brands and identifying an opportunity.

For one of the projects, we explored different software and method to ideate and build various prototypes for the vision. For context, this course project is to pick a brand and identify an opportunity to design for it, and I decided on Discord. However, during class, the instructor will assign another brand to mix things up and create some interesting outcomes, which I was assigned with Apple. The brands are purposely selected as unusual partners.

Sketching and moving in 3D spaces and designing assets using... well, VR.

To quickly sketch in 3D, we explored Gravity Sketch in VR to generate various spaces and assets. It was my first time using Gravity Sketch and VR, so getting used to the controls was quite a journey. As I was going for a cubic look without snapping, I spent two weeks hard-twisting my arms, just trying to place things right. However, I discovered the grid feature, which turned the whole experience upside down and significantly sped up the process. It was a unique learning experience.

Using Blender to fine-tune and add details to the ideas.

Crafting materials that embrace the brands and elevate the experience.

Once the assets were designed, we explored variations of materials that can bring out the brands. We used Substance as it allowed us to quickly apply materials with all the freedom to customize and craft our own. I never needed to make my own (usually, we used presets), so the knobs and sliders were quite confusing. Adding exciting variations was also challenging since Apple is quite simple. I went through took many rounds of revision and experimentation to nail everything,

The rewarding process of finding, trying and troubleshooting.

Although there were many issues along the way, I really enjoyed the journey of troubleshooting and trying out different options to make things work. I especially enjoy this kind of process - constant googling and experimenting. It’s the fastest way to learn a new tool, and the feeling of accomplishment is fantastic once you figure it all out.

Experimenting with AI images for unorthodox inspirations.

Although sometimes controversial, I still tested with AI images to generate ideas that may help with the project. I tested out with Midjourney and DALL-E to compare the differences. It felt like a search engine, but the images are by AI rather than other artists. I only used a few minor tweaks with my shape design and lighting.

Translating imagination into text for the system and comparing the style between services.

Working with AI was eye-opening but also challenging. Using text rather than uploading images is suggested to generate images that diverge from your current project. Translating the visuals in your mind into text was quite fascinating but also challenging. For the most part, you'll have to curate a list of words that fit the AI's understanding. It was festinating as different services interpret the terms differently. The style was also quite different between Midjourney and DALL-E. Although Midjourney has a more artistic feel, I have to physically type in 3D rendering to get the result I'm looking for. As for DALL-E, it focuses more on 3D.

Exploring new ways to present 3D assets more effectively.

Experiencing the space with an interactive prototype.

Although Sketchfab allowed us to rotate and scale the different models, nothing is more fun than experiencing the space. So, I built an interactive prototype using Unreal Engine.

The importing process could have been smoother than Sketchfab. I must manually set colliders, convert materials and textures, readjust the light, and “program” all the controls. It was tough, but hey, overcoming all those challenges was a delightful process.

Prototype Demo.

Utilizing Mixed reality and Unity to prototype and express ideas quickly.

Exploring the connection between the physical world and the virtual.

The second project I want to highlight is a mixed reality project offering different experiences for various transportation modes, including pedestrian, micro-mobility, automobile, and transit. The project encourages us to explore the connection between the physical and virtual worlds. With Kiran, we built a modular steering wheel that uses the tracking of the Oculus controller to sync the steering movement in the Unity prototype.

We seek to build a broader perspective for people.

Building, testing, and iterating to connect the physical with the virtual simulation.

The process was quite simple - building, play-testing, and iterating. As both the physical and virtual are connected, teamwork is crucial for all the parts to run synchronously, so we set a time every week to only focus on building and connecting the prototypes. We used Unity for the virtual experience the set up the traffic simulation and paper and wood for the physical.

Personal takeaways.

Being on top of things about new technology and software can help optimize the creative process. Plus, it’s always fun to try new things.

New technology like AI images shouldn’t be seen as a replacement for a designer but as a new tool for us to use

Once you only rely on the results the tools provide, you become a spectator that watches instead of a designer that creates.
