Neuron.

A six months internship in a design agency working on various clients’ projects.

The most incredible experience of working with kind and welcoming designers.

Neuron UX is a UI/UX design agency based in San Francisco that focuses on designing B2B workplace products. Interning there was one of the greatest gifts in 2022 since I was scrambling since one of my offers was blocked by the sudden COVID restrictions in Japan. Neuron was swift during the process, and it only took me two weeks to receive an offer. It was one of the best design experiences. Everyone was kind and welcoming, and I was immediately trusted with client projects in production and presentation.

Timeline.

Spring & Summer 2022.

Skill.

UIUX design, visual design, iconography.

Software.

Mainly Figma, others design software as support.

Contribution.

Collaborated with teams of designers and clients.

A fully-remote team with designers from the US, Canada, and Europe.

Worked through four client projects, each with its unique characteristics and challenges.

Throughout my time at Neuron, I worked on four client projects and some marketing materials for Neuron. Each task had its unique aspect; some involved strategy and visual design, while others required UX research. However, I enjoyed every project as each challenged me to expand my design skillsets.

Life at Neuron

A fascinating trip to SF meeting the team - Neuron Summer Jamboree 2022.

Meeting everyone and hosted a public Meetup event in person.

The first day was the most important. It was a Meetup event held by Neuron to present the Top 10 UX trends for 2022 in person. The presentation was a collaboration between two UX leads, Chris and Matt, and myself over two months. The event was public and was also streamed on YouTube. Personal takeaways.

Never be afraid to ask questions and share your thoughts. You may be surprised at how much you and your peers can learn.

The biggest skill I’ve improved is making quick changes and clearly presenting the design variations to the clients.

Working in a small team, you’re not just working for yourself, but the team. As a result, communication and self-discipline.
