Airbnb.

Redesigning the corporate brand identity of Airbnb.

A rebranding project that follows a design brief based on visual audit and research.

This is a course project to rebrand Airbnb. I began by researching and identifying challenges for the company to create a design brief with brand statements, personalities, and considerations. Then, based on the brief, we ideated and developed a new brand system. The new brand concept is to showcase how you can visit anywhere in the world through Airbnb's portal.

Timeline.

Fall 2021.

Skill.

Brand design.

Software.

Illustrator, Photoshop, XD, After Effects, Figma.

Contribution.

Individual project.

A combination of lighthearted, dependable, and adventurous in the brand logo.

The final brand logo and type combines the three personality traits from the brief - lighthearted, dependable, and adventurous. The welcoming circle represents the portal to bring people anywhere, and the city and mountain outline brings out the adventurous side of Airbnb. The bolded type was a way to add dependableness.

Brand color.

While the primary color is very similar to the original brand as the pinkish-red, I chose cyan and orange as supporting colors for the different temperatures. The overall color is bright to showcase the excitement of traveling.

Brand typography.

I chose Gilroy as the primary typeface since it is a versatile and welcoming San serif typeface with optimum clarity. In addition, it is a perfect typeface for headlines, subtitles, and body copy. On the other hand, Roboto Slab is chosen to support and contrast Gilroy as it has a mechanical skeleton, and the forms are essentially geometric. However, at the same time, it features friendly and open curves that bring out a lighthearted personality.

Brand photography.

I curated a set of photography styles for different categories, which included details, architectures, experiences, and activities. The color in the photos is toned down to contrast with the bright color palette.

Graphical elements.

To emphasize the concept of a portal, layers of translucent circles are stacked on each other to create the feeling of entering a portal. Moreover, the scaling and positioning of the treatment can also be varied as it can be scaled up and only show portions of the circle.

Brand application.

Understand the current brand and identify the existing issues.

Compile and visualize the different personalities.

To begin the brainstorming process, I curated a set of words and gathered images related to each attribute. With this, I can get an overview of three traits and combine elements to form other concepts.

Exploring concepts and designing variations.

With sketching, I explored concepts of a floating island to present the idea of being anywhere, a treehouse representing a stable living space, and a window or portal that can be open to anything.

Personal takeaways.

How a design is interpreted often depends on how the delivery. It can be a great direction, but if you fail to present it, clients will never choose that direction.

When designing a brand, you need to look at all aspects (color, shape, type, photo, etc.) to ensure everything aligns with the personality of the client brief.

You always want to try different styles when sketching ideas. In my case, I often need help focusing only on flat design. So it was refreshing to try perspectives and other styles.
