Angine.

A virtual audio mixer that allows users to set up, manage, and save audio settings.

A course project inspired from my frustration when setting audio for my home studio.

Angine is a course project to design and build a marketable product. My frustration inspired the project of setting up my audio system when I was trying to set up a home studio. As a tribute to my pain, I designed a virtual audio mixer that optimized setting up, managing, and saving audio settings to handle multiple audio devices and tasks.

Separating everything into tracks for free-ranges of control and management.

Separating everything into tracks to manage audio freely.

Angine was inspired by physical mixers and DAWs. The system separates everything into its tracks, which allows free audio routing. The track is grouped into audio devices connected to your computer and audio source played on your computer. By grouping, users can have complete control in assigning audio sources to devices as desired.

Providing organizational tools for the complex and overwhelming system.

This product is for heavy users, so providing organizational tools to help with total freedom is crucial. Just like how designers like to deal with layers, right? Users may assign a color and name each track to their liking, which can avoid confusion caused by the daunting list and baffling device names.

Transform settings into presets to bridge between devices and streamline daily activities.

Angine includes a preset system allowing settings to be saved locally and by cloud to accounts. This way, users only need to set it once and will be able to maintain it across multiple platforms. Moreover, users can toggle between different presets to have the best setting to fit various tasks like gaming, producing, etc.

Understanding the competitors and the needs of the users.

To get a better understanding of the market landscape. I first dug into the competitors identifying their similarities, differences, strengths, and weaknesses to better position my design. I then conducted interviews targeting those with heavy audio needs, like streamers, to understand the different approaches and frustrations.

Building a design system to ensure the product scalability.

To ensure the product can scale, I documented a detailed design system, which is essential in the long run. The system includes the usage of color, typography, grid, icons, and responsive design for future development.

Personal takeaways.

Complicated UI requires repeated tests and iteration to get it just right. Sometimes testing is more valuable than following conventions because only some work the same.

Building a design system is crucial for the scalability of a product. Even though this project only scratches the surface, that’s a valuable learning process for me.

Design and business are strongly tied together. It doesn’t matter if your product is design-driven or business-driven. Every factor will affect the decision and project directions.
