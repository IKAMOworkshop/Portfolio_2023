Nebula.

A smart ecosystem that learns and adapts to provide the best sleep routine.

A mock design studio course focusing on solving design problems for a client brief.

Nebula is a course project in a mock studio environment where we propose a design solution responding to a client brief. Our brief was to address the relationship between technology and mental hygiene. Our team focused on sleep as research shows it significantly affects people’s mental well-being.‍

We designed a smart sleep trainer that learns, adapts, and responds to the user’s routine to assist individuals in getting quality sleep.

Understand and identify elements for an ideal sleep cycle.

We first want to understand the topic and problems by doing secondary research and talking to our primary users - young adults. Through research, we can break down a sleep cycle into three main parts - before, during, and after. We then identify the ideal situation for the best quality sleep for each section. For example, people need a 30-minute cool down before, a cool, dark room around 60°F during, and waking up at the end of a REM cycle. <

The app utilizes existing data from user’s phone to provide adaptive suggestions.

The app collects existing data from the user’s phone, including calendar events and other paired devices. The app can then utilize the data to create adaptive suggestions and present the best plan. Of course, if you’re trying to hit a deadline, the app will adapt to it for the following day.

Combining the two to modify the environment for an immersive experience.

With the two combined, Nebula can provide suggestions and environmental changes before, during, and after sleep. Moreover, if you have other paired devices like a smart thermostat or room speaker, you can have those as part of your journey to a night of quality sleep.

While the app collects, the product works together to create the experience.

Pinpointing the needs and iterating the prototypes.

To kickoff the project research, we sent out a survey to gather data and interview some primary target users to find a starting point. As the project moved forward, we constantly tested our ideas and prototypes with users to ensure the clarity and feasibility of Nebula.

We surveyed 34 people between the age 18-31.
